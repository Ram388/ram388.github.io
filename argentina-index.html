Argentina

Introduction

After declaring independence from Span in 1816, Uruguay, Bolivia, and Paraguay seperated into their own respective countries. The area that remained would come to be known as Argentina. Between 1860 and 1930, most of the newcomers traveled to the land came from Italy and Spain, later resulting in the rich culture that is now known throughout the world today. Prior to the 20th century, Argentina's history contained many different periods of internal political unrest along with several disputes between both the military factions and civillians.
